Key Ratios & Metrics

Once you extract raw numbers from financial statements, compute ratios to compare companies. Common ratios:

  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings): Market Price per Share ÷ Earnings per Share (EPS).
    • A high P/E may indicate growth expectations; a low P/E could signal undervaluation or trouble.
  • P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book): Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share.
    • Book Value = (Total Assets − Total Liabilities) ÷ Shares Outstanding.
    • A P/B below 1 might indicate the stock trades below net assets; above 1 may imply premium valuation.
  • ROE (Return on Equity): Net Income ÷ Shareholder Equity.
    • Measures profitability relative to invested equity. Healthy Pakistani banks often target ROE > 15%.
  • Dividend Yield: Annual Dividend per Share ÷ Market Price per Share.
    • Investors seeking income often look for yields > 5% from stable companies.

Example: MCB Bank has EPS of PKR 15, stock price PKR 200 → P/E = 13.3×. If Book Value = PKR 100, P/B = 2×. If dividend = PKR 10 per share, yield = 5% on PKR 200 stock price.

Tip: Compare ratios within the same industry—banking vs. textile ratios can differ drastically; direct cross-sector comparisons may mislead.

Key Terminology

When navigating PSX, you’ll encounter various terms:

  • Ticker Symbol: Unique code for each listed company (e.g., HBL for Habib Bank Limited).
  • Bid & Ask: Bid = highest price a buyer is willing to pay; Ask = lowest price a seller will accept. The difference is called the spread.
  • Order Types:
    • Market Order: Buy/sell at the best available price immediately.
    • Limit Order: Buy/sell at a specified price or better; may not execute if price never reached.
    • Stop-Loss Order: Automatically sells a stock when it reaches a certain (lower) price to limit losses.
  • Indices:
    • KSE-100: Tracks top 100 companies by market capitalization—PSX’s benchmark.
    • KSE-30: Tracks top 30 large-cap companies.
    • KMI-30: Tracks 30 Shariah-compliant companies.
  • T+2 Settlement: Transaction date plus two business days for funds/shares to exchange hands.

Tip: Understanding these basics helps you read the order book, interpret index moves, and place orders accurately.

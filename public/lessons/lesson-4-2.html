Common Indicators

Indicators help quantify trends and momentum. Widely used ones:

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA): Average of closing prices over N periods (e.g., 50-day SMA). If price crosses above 50-day SMA, it might signal bullish momentum.
  • Exponential Moving Average (EMA): Similar to SMA, but gives more weight to recent prices (e.g., 12-day & 26-day EMAs used in MACD).
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): Oscillator between 0–100.
    • RSI > 70 = overbought (potential pullback).
    • RSI < 30 = oversold (potential rebound).
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): Difference between 12-day EMA and 26-day EMA.
    • The “signal” line (9-day EMA of MACD) helps identify crossovers (bullish/bearish).
  • Bollinger Bands: SMA ± 2 standard deviations.
    • When bands contract (low volatility), a breakout often follows.

Example: On a daily chart of MCB Bank, if the 14-day RSI dips below 30 and then rises above 30, that may signal a potential buy opportunity from an oversold condition.

Tip: Combine indicators (e.g., RSI + MACD) for confirmation rather than relying on one alone.

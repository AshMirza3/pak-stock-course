Reading Financial Statements

Fundamental analysis begins with understanding a company’s financial statements. Three key statements:

  • Income Statement: Tracks revenue, expenses, and profit over a period (quarterly/annually). Key line items:
    • Revenue (sales)
    • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
    • Operating Expenses (Opex)
    • Net Profit (after tax)
  • Balance Sheet: Snapshot of assets, liabilities, and equity at a point in time. Key line items:
    • Assets (current & non-current)
    • Liabilities (current & non-current)
    • Equity (share capital, retained earnings)
  • Cash Flow Statement: Shows actual cash inflows/outflows in operating, investing, and financing activities. Helps you verify that “profit” translates into real cash generation.

Example (HBL Ltd.):

  • Revenue increased from PKR 300 billion to PKR 350 billion year-on-year.
  • Net profit margin stabilized around 20%, indicating strong cost control.
  • Operating cash flow was positive PKR 50 billion, showing healthy liquidity.

Key takeaway: Always cross-verify net profit with operating cash flow—high profits on paper may not reflect actual cash generation if receivables rise excessively.

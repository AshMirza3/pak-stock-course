Top Pakistani Mutual Fund Houses

Several asset management companies dominate Pakistan’s mutual fund industry:

  • Lakson Investments: Pakistan’s largest independent asset manager with 7 open-ended mutual funds, including both conventional and Shariah-compliant. Known for strong corporate governance and research-driven strategies.
  • MCB Arif Habib Savings & Investments (MCB-AH): One of the oldest and largest AMCs, offering equity, income, and money market funds with solid performance track records.
  • UBL Fund Managers: Offers a diverse suite of funds, including UBL Stock Advantage Fund (USAF) and UBL Liquidity Plus (ULP). Renowned for low expense ratios.
  • Alfalah GHP Investment Management (AGP): Known for Shariah-compliant solutions; they manage AGP Income Fund, AGP Islamic Stock Fund, and more.
  • NAFA (National Asset Management): Joint venture of NBP, Faysal Bank, and Fidelity International; offers a variety of equity, fixed income, and capital protected funds.

Comparison Factors:

  • Fund Performance: Review 1-year, 3-year, 5-year NAV growth vs. KSE-100 index.
  • Expense Ratio: Lower fees can significantly boost net returns over time.
  • Minimum Investment: Some funds require as low as PKR 5,000; others have PKR 25,000 or PKR 50,000 minimums.
  • Dividend Policy: Some distribute semi-annual dividends, others reinvest automatically (growth mode).

Tip: For conservative investors, money market funds or income funds (e.g., MCB Cash Management Optimizer) may be preferable to equity-heavy offerings.

Chart Types and Patterns

Technical analysis uses price charts to identify patterns. Common chart types:

  • Line Chart: Plots closing prices over time. Simple, but lacks intra-day detail.
  • Bar Chart: Each bar shows open, high, low, close (OHLC) for a period.
    • Top of the bar = high, bottom = low, tick on left = open, tick on right = close.
  • Candlestick Chart: Similar to bar charts, but uses a “body” for open/close and “wicks” for high/low.
    • Bullish Candle: Closing price > opening price (often colored green).
    • Bearish Candle: Closing price < opening price (often colored red).

Basic Patterns:

  • Support & Resistance: Horizontal levels where price repeatedly bounces (support) or fails to breach (resistance).
  • Double Top / Double Bottom: Reversal patterns—two peaks (double top) or two troughs (double bottom).
  • Head & Shoulders: Reversal pattern: left shoulder, head (higher peak), right shoulder.
  • Trendlines: Diagonal lines connecting successive lows in an uptrend or highs in a downtrend.

Example: On a daily candlestick chart of HBL, a round of three candles forming a “head & shoulders” near PKR 150 might signal a bearish reversal toward PKR 140 support.

Asset Allocation for Pakistani Investors

Asset allocation is dividing your portfolio among different asset classes—equities, fixed income, cash, and possibly commodities. In Pakistan, typical retail investors focus heavily on equities; however, a balanced allocation may look like:

  • Equities (40%–60%): Direct stocks or equity mutual funds (LEF, AGP Islamic Stock Fund).
  • Fixed Income/Bonds (20%–30%): Government bonds, corporate Sukuk, income funds (LIF).
  • Money Market/Cash (10%–20%): Money Market Funds (LMMF, ULP) or bank fixed deposits.
  • Commodities (0%–10%): Gold exposure—via physical, PKR-denominated gold contracts, or gold ETFs (e.g., PSX’s KSE Gold Index).

Factors to consider:

  • Age & Risk Tolerance: Younger investors can afford more equity risk; retirees may prefer fixed income and cash.
  • Investment Horizon: For a 5+ year horizon, equities fetch higher returns (~15%–20% historically), whereas bonds yield ~12%–14%.
  • Inflation: Pakistan’s inflation often >10%. Aim for at least inflation+3% real return by mixing equities and income instruments.
  • Currency Risk: If you hold foreign assets (e.g., US stocks), you’re exposed to PKR depreciation risk. Most retail investors stick to PKR assets to avoid FX risk.

Sample Portfolio: A 30-year-old might have:

  • 60% equities (30% direct stocks, 30% equity mutual funds)
  • 25% fixed income (corporate bonds, income funds)
  • 10% money market funds
  • 5% gold exposure

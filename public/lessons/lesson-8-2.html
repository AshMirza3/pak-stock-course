Venture Capital & Private Equity in Pakistan

Beyond public markets, Pakistani startups and medium-sized enterprises raise capital via venture capital (VC) and private equity (PE). Lakson’s VC arm and other players include:

  • Lakson Venture Capital: Focuses on early-stage tech startups—FinTech, e-commerce, healthtech. Provides not only capital but mentorship and network access.
  • i2i Ventures: Karachi-based VC focusing on pre-seed and seed rounds—invests in tech founders across Pakistan.
  • Karandaaz Pakistan: A public-private partnership that provides risk capital for SMEs and women entrepreneurs.
  • Pak-n-Tel Ventures: Corporate VC arm of PTCL, investing in telecom and digital services startups.

How VC/PE Investments Work:

  1. Seed Round: Startups raise PKR 5–20 million in exchange for 5%–15% equity. Early backers take high risk for potentially >10× returns.
  2. Series A/B: After product-market fit is proven, Series A (~PKR 50 million+) or Series B (~PKR 200 million+) rounds focus on scaling operations.
  3. Exit: VCs exit via acquisition by a larger company, secondary share sale, or IPO (on PSX if company grows large enough—rare but possible, e.g., Systems Ltd.’s spinout).

Case in Point: Lakson VC invested PKR 20 million in FinTech startup “Tabadul Pay” in 2021 (Seed Round). By 2024, Tabadul Pay had processed PKR 15 billion in transactions—Lakson’s stake appreciated ~5× in valuation. In 2025, Tabadul plans to apply for an IPO on PSX to raise additional growth capital.

Tip: VC/PE carries very high risk. Limited partners (LPs) commit capital for 5–7 years before an exit. Retail investors can get indirect exposure via PE-focused mutual funds (e.g., Lakson’s Private Equity Fund).

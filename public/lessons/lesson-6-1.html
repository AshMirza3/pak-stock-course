Diversification Concepts

Diversification is the practice of spreading investments across different asset classes, sectors, or geographies to reduce risk. In Pakistan, sector concentrations can be high, so a diversified approach helps mitigate idiosyncratic risk.

Why it matters:

  • Owning only banking stocks: If regulatory changes or an economic slowdown hit banks, your entire portfolio suffers.
  • Spreading across industries (banking, textile, energy, consumer goods) helps cushion against sector-specific shocks.
  • Geographic diversification: While most Pakistani retail investors stay local, investing in a mutual fund with some exposure to international securities can further reduce risk.

Example: Suppose you have PKR 100,000. Instead of putting it all in one bank stock like HBL, you could allocate:

  • 30% in banking (e.g., HBL, MCB)
  • 20% in textile exporters (e.g., Nishat Mills)
  • 20% in energy (e.g., Hub Power Co.)
  • 15% in consumer goods (e.g., Unilever Pakistan)
  • 15% in a money market fund (e.g., LMMF) for stability.

Tip: Avoid “putting all eggs in one basket”—choose at least 4–6 sectors, or invest via a balanced mutual fund for automatic diversification.

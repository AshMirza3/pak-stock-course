Using SIPs & Systematic Investment Plans

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (e.g., monthly) into a mutual fund. SIPs harness rupee‐cost averaging, which smooths out market volatility.

How SIP works:

  • You choose an equity or balanced fund (e.g., Lakson Equity Fund).
  • You commit PKR 5,000 every month—regardless of net asset value (NAV).
  • When markets dip, PKR 5,000 buys more units (lower NAV); when markets rise, fewer units (higher NAV). Over time, average cost per unit stabilizes.

Benefits:

  • Disciplined Investing: Forces saving and investing consistently.
  • Rupee-Cost Averaging: Mitigates impact of short-term volatility.
  • Compounding: Monthly returns reinvested build a larger corpus over 5–10 years.

Example Scenario: If you start a PKR 5,000/month SIP in Lakson Equity Fund in January 2021 at NAV PKR 10 (500 units), and the NAV falls to PKR 8 in June (625 units), by December NAV = PKR 12 (417 units). Your total units after 12 months might be ~6,000 units. If NAV in January 2022 is PKR 15, corpus = 6,000 × 15 = PKR 90,000, even though you invested only PKR 60,000.

Tip: Start early and remain invested—SIPs work best over 5+ years for equity funds due to market cycles.

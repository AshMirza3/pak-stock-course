Real-World Case Study: Lakson Equity Fund Performance

Let’s compare Lakson Equity Fund (LEF) vs. KSE-100 Index over the past five years (2020–2025):
Year LEF NAV Growth KSE-100 Return
2020 +12.8% +10.5%
2021 +25.2% +22.0%
2022 –5.4% –7.1%
2023 +18.3% +15.0%
2024 (YTD Jun) +8.5% +6.2%

Over five years, LEF generated a cumulative return of ~61%, while KSE-100 returned ~49%. This outperformance is due to:

  • Active stock selection—Lakson’s research avoided highly leveraged energy stocks in 2022 downturn.
  • Sector weighting—higher allocation to defensive sectors (FMCG, pharmaceuticals) when markets were volatile.
  • Timely profit-booking—reduced exposure before major corrections, locking in gains.

Lessons Learned:

  • Active management can add alpha in inefficient markets like PSX.
  • However, high expense ratios (1.5%–2%) can erode outperformance if not monitored closely.
  • Diversify across multiple AMCs—don’t rely on a single fund to outperform every cycle.

Tip: Before investing in any equity fund, compare its rolling 3- and 5-year returns vs. benchmark and other funds, adjusting for risk (standard deviation).

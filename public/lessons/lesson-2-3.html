Brokerage Houses & How to Choose One

To trade on PSX, you must open an account with a licensed brokerage. Key considerations:

  • Reputation & Track Record: Look for established firms:
    • Lakson Investments: Pakistan’s largest independent asset manager, also offering brokerage services via affiliates. Known for strong research coverage and robust online platform.
    • JS Global Capital: Provides TraderPlus platform—popular for its user-friendly interface and research reports.
    • AKD Securities: Emphasizes advisory services and corporate finance; strong institutional client base.
    • Arif Habib Securities: Highly liquid order book; one of the oldest brokerage houses in Pakistan.
  • Brokerage Fees & Commissions: Typically a percentage of trade value (often 0.035%–0.05% each side), plus SECP fee, Exchange Fee, and VAT. Compare total cost structures across brokers.
  • Technology & Platform: If you prefer online trading, evaluate mobile/web platforms for real‐time quotes, charting, order placement, and ease-of-use.
  • Research & Advisory: Some brokers include daily market analysis, sector reports, and stock recommendations—useful for beginners.
  • Customer Support & Branch Network: In-person support can be helpful for new investors; check if there’s a local branch near you.

How to Open an Account:

  1. Fill out a KYC (Know Your Customer) form—provide CNIC, proof of address, and a passport-size photo.
  2. Submit a copy of CDC investor account form (available online or at branch).
  3. Deposit initial margin (often PKR 10,000 minimum) into your brokerage account.
  4. Receive login credentials for your online trading portal, or paper contract notes if transacting offline.

Tip: If you want Shariah-compliant trading, ensure the broker offers screening against Shariah-compliant lists (e.g., KMI-30 constituents).

IPO Investing in Pakistan

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is when a private company issues shares to the public for the first time. In Pakistan, companies often raise capital via IPOs through a book-building process or a fixed‐price offering.

How to Apply for an IPO:

  1. Obtain an IPO form or download from the PSX website (under “Public Issues”).
  2. Fill in your CDC participant ID, account details, and provide CNIC for verification.
  3. Submit the form to your broker’s office or online portal (if broker offers e-IPO facility).
  4. Pay subscription money (e.g., PKR 10/share if issue price is PKR 10; minimum lots may apply).
  5. After the issuance period closes, shares are allotted based on demand (often via lottery if oversubscribed).
  6. If allotted, shares appear in your CDC account; if not, subscription money is returned net of bank charges.

Key Terms:

  • Book Building: Institutional investors bid within a price range. Retail investors often subscribe at the floor price.
  • Fixed-Price Offer: Issue price is predetermined; all investors subscribe at that price.
  • Depository Account: Shares are credited to your CDC account—no physical certificates. You need a CDS account to hold shares.

Example: In March 2025, ABC Foods launched an IPO at PKR 15/share with a floor of 15 and cap of 17 (book building). The issue was 5× oversubscribed by retail. Allotted at PKR 16. Retail investors who applied for 1,000 shares likely received ~200 shares via lottery, debited PKR 3,200 (1,000 × 16 × 80% provisional), with balance refunded.

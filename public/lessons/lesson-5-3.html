Lakson’s Mutual Fund Offerings

Lakson Investments manages seven open-ended mutual funds. Here’s a snapshot:

  • Lakson Money Market Fund (LMMF):
    • Invests in government treasury bills, commercial paper, and short-term deposits.
    • Objective: Preserve capital and deliver stable returns (~12%–14% annual yield).
  • Lakson Income Fund (LIF):
    • Fixed-income fund—mix of government bonds, corporate debt.
    • Target yield: ~13%–15% per annum.
  • Lakson Equity Fund (LEF):
    • Long-only equity portfolio, primarily investing in large-caps (Banks, Cements, Oil & Gas).
    • Benchmark: KSE-100. Often aims to outperform over a 3–5 year horizon.
  • Lakson Shariah** (Shariah-compliant equity fund):
    • Invests only in Shariah-approved stocks (e.g., companies in KMI-30 index).
    • Meets guidelines on debt ratio, interest income threshold.
  • Lakson REIT Index Fund (LRIF):
    • Passive fund tracking the PSX REIT Index—exposure to real estate investment trusts.
  • Lakson GHP Equity Active Fund (LEAF):
    • Actively managed, balanced portfolio combining equities and fixed-income securities.
  • Lakson Fixed Income Fund (LFIF):
    • Blends high-yield corporate bonds, government paper, and money-market instruments.

How to Invest in Lakson Funds:

  1. Visit Lakson Investments’ official website and select the desired fund.
  2. Complete the online application form (KYC documents required: CNIC, proof of residence).
  3. Pay the minimum amount (often PKR 5,000 or PKR 25,000 depending on fund).
  4. Receive confirmation of unit allocation; track NAV updates daily via Lakson’s portal.
  5. To redeem, submit a redemption request; proceeds settle within T+2 days.

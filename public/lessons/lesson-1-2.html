History of the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX)

The PSX, as it exists today, is the result of a 2016 merger between three regional exchanges: the Karachi Stock Exchange (KSE), Lahore Stock Exchange (LSE), and Islamabad Stock Exchange (ISE). The consolidation aimed to unify regulations, reduce operational costs, and improve market depth.

Key milestones:

  • 1947: Karachi Stock Exchange (originally established in the 1930s under British rule) becomes a major trading venue post-independence.
  • 1977: Nationalization period under Zulfikar Ali Bhutto’s government led to state control of many brokerages, slowing market growth.
  • 1991 onward: Privatization and liberalization fueled renewed investor interest. KSE 100 Index launched to benchmark top companies.
  • 2016: KSE, LSE, and ISE merge into a single Pakistan Stock Exchange. Demutualization completed in 2017—members own shares in PSX’s operator.

Result: Today, PSX is the country’s only exchange, trading equities, debt securities, and derivatives with over 550 listed companies and daily volumes often exceeding PKR 20 billion.
